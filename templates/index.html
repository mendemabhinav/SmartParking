    How It Works: Greedy Algorithm & Priority Queues

    The Greedy Choice: Instead of searching the entire parking lot for an empty space, this system uses a Data Structure called a Min-Heap (Priority Queue). Available slots are weighted by their distance to the entrance. The algorithm "greedily" pops the lowest-weighted slot off the top of the queue in $O(1)$ time, guaranteeing the closest spot instantly.

    Reservations & Conflict Resolution: When a user reserves a spot online, the backend removes that slot from the Min-Heap so the Greedy algorithm cannot assign it to a drive-up customer. A background thread runs a countdown. Once the time expires (or the vehicle leaves), the slot is pushed back into the priority queue, self-healing the grid.
